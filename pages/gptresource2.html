Recognition Across Skin Tones

Core signs like erythema and scaling look different depending on skin tone. Adjust the controls to see variations.

Common Regional Patterns

Subtle erythema or lichenification around eyes and mouth. More texture-based cues in darker tones.
Pruritic patches in the elbows; rubbing causes thickened skin. Important to differentiate from contact triggers.

Skin of Color Considerations

On darker tones, erythema may appear purple, gray, or ashen instead of bright red. Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation is common and should not be mistaken for active flare. Skin of Color Society
Scaling may appear lighter (ashy) on dark skin, requiring careful texture evaluation. DermNet
