Learn About Eczema

Eczema (atopic dermatitis) is a common, chronic skin condition that causes inflammation, itchiness, and irritation. It affects people of all ages and skin tones—but it doesn’t look the same on everyone.

TONO was created to help make eczema easier to recognize—especially in skin of color. That starts with understanding what to look for, how it may appear differently across tones, and why clinical representation matters.

Why Representation Matters

Most dermatology references show eczema on light skin. In reality, signs of inflammation—like redness—may appear violet, gray, dark brown, or not visible at all in darker tones.

Clinical Features to Look For

Eczema has a wide range of appearances depending on age, severity, chronicity, and skin tone. Here are some features you may encounter:

Redness from inflammation. In darker skin, it may appear purple, gray, or hard to detect.

Thickened, leathery skin from chronic scratching. Often hyperpigmented in dark skin.

Surface-level scratches or wounds from intense itching.

Dry, flaky skin—may appear ashy in richly pigmented skin.

Darker patches left after inflammation heals.

Lighter areas of skin caused by disrupted melanin production after inflammation.

Eczema in Different Skin Tones

  • Light skin: Red, scaly patches are usually easy to spot.
  • Medium skin: May appear reddish-brown or dusky with pigment changes.
  • Deep skin: Inflammation may look purple, gray, or may not show at all.
