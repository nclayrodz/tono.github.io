Learn About Eczema

Eczema (atopic dermatitis) is a common, chronic skin condition that causes inflammation, itchiness, and irritation. It affects people of all ages and skin tones—but it doesn’t look the same on everyone.

TONO was created to help make eczema easier to recognize—especially in skin of color. That starts with understanding what to look for, how it may appear differently across tones, and why clinical representation matters.

Why Representation Matters

Most dermatology references show eczema on light skin. In reality, signs of inflammation—like redness—may appear violet, gray, dark brown, or not visible at all in darker tones. Post-inflammatory changes, like hyperpigmentation or hypopigmentation, are also more prominent and longer lasting in richly pigmented skin.

TONO’s images show the same eczema condition across different skin tones and severities, making it easier to:

  • See how eczema presents in a range of people
  • Compare features across tones
  • Avoid underdiagnosis or misdiagnosis in patients with darker skin

Clinical Features to Look For

Eczema has a wide range of appearances depending on age, severity, chronicity, and skin tone. Here are some features you may encounter:

Feature Description
ErythemaRedness from inflammation. May appear purple, gray, or hard to detect in darker skin tones. Redness or inflammation, which may be less visible in dark skin.
LichenificationThickened, leathery skin caused by chronic rubbing or scratching. Often appears hyperpigmented. Thickened, leathery skin from scratching.
ExcoriationScratch marks or open wounds caused by itching. Surface damage caused by scratching.
XerosisDry, flaky skin. Can appear “ashy” in richly pigmented skin. Very dry, flaky skin.
HyperpigmentationDarkened patches left after inflammation heals. Darker discoloration after healing.
HypopigmentationLightened patches where pigment has been lost. Lighter discoloration post-inflammation.

Eczema in Different Skin Tones

Eczema looks different across the spectrum of skin tones:

  • Light skin: Red, scaly patches are usually easy to spot.
  • Medium skin: May appear reddish-brown or dusky with pigment changes.
  • Deep skin: Inflammation can appear purple, gray, or may not show at all.
